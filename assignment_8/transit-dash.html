Transit Dash

I created a data dashboard to address transit inequity in Pittsburgh.

Context/Problem

In my interaction design studio class, our team had to design a data dashboard for two given, transit related personas. The dashboard and personas had to be related to the new e-scooter rollout in Pittsburgh.

Process

Research

We were given the personas at the outset so the focus on this assignment was the design part. For research, we analyzed some basic transit data given in a spreadsheet to explore potential avenues of data displays.

Design

Our given personas had differing needs. One of them was Paula, a transit advocate that wanted to understand the e-scooter rollout impact on accessibility, both negative and positive.

Another was Eliot, a policy analyst that wanted to ensure mobility for all Pittsburghers as well as making sure that the e-scooter rollout was equitable across different neighborhoods.

As a team, we considered which data widgets would be most useful for both transit advocates. We also drew upon basic data dashboard design principles like having a clear typographic hierarchy, strong, logical labels and controlled use of color to draw focus to key areas.

Solution

Our final dashboard solution included a map widget with accessibility overlays so that Paula could see if a scooter was blocking a pedestrian walkway. It also shows areas with low mobility options so that Eliot can advocate for more e-scooter distribution to those areas. Furthermore, we had a graph widget to display relevant trends from the map over a long period of time, and we had a mock Twitter API so that both Paula and Eliot could get real-time feedback on what people thought of the e-scooters.

In conclusion, our team designed a robust data dashboard that met the needs of our assigned personas.
